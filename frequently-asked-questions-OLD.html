We have built-in combination locks on our lockers and would like to change the combinations. Do you have instructions on how to do that?

How to change the combinations on models 1630, 1631, 1654, 1655, 1670 and 1671:
  1. Insert control key and turn it right one-quarter turn. Open door
  2. Revolve dial to the right at least one complete turn, stopping at zero
  3. Depress push-button on back of lock case and hold in depressed position
  4. With other hand, turn dial right to number 48. Do not turn dial more than two numbers with button depressed. Then release push button
  5. Turn dial to the right until you hear a click, then stop
  6. Close locker door and remove key by turning it one-quarter left, back to its original position
  7. Combination has now been changed automatically to the next combination assigned. Dial the new combination (refer to chart for dialing numbers)
  8. If a mistake was made in following above instructions, dial first combination (see chart). If it does not work, dial next combination, etc., until correct combination to open lock is reached

IMPORTANT: Make sure the combination change is reported to the lock supervisor

What is the dialing sequence to open a standard combination lock?

Please follow the steps outlined below:
  1. Turn the dial 3 times to the right and stop on the first number of the sequence
  2. Turn the dial to the left, pass the first number of the sequence and stop on the second number.
  3. Turn the dial to the right and stop on the third number of the sequence
  4. Pull up on the shackle

How do I open a high security combination lock model 2002 or 2010?

Please follow the steps outlined below:
  1. Turn the dial 3 times to the right and stop on the first number of the sequence
  2. Turn the dial to the left, pass the second number of the sequence and stop on it the second time around
  3. Turn the dial to the right and stop on the third number of the sequence
  4. Turn the dial to the left until it stops
  5. Hold dial at stop and pull up on shackle to open lock.

How can I fix a brass resettable combination lock that sticks from time to time?

We recommend lubricating your lock with a dry lubricant, such as: LPS, TRI FLOW, KRYTOX RFE Advanced PTFE, or Elmer's Slide-All. NEVER use an oil or dry graphite lubricant because oil attracts dust and it can also interfere with the movement of the locking mechanism

My school purchased built-in combination locks for our lockers, but our maintenance staff does not know how to install them. Do you have instructions you can send to me?

Installation Instructions (1630, 1631, 1652, 1653, 1654, 1655, 1670, and 1671)
  1. Before separating front and back assemblies, remove combination tag and write locker number in area designated. This is important!
  2. Set dial at "0" at red marker. This setting aligns interior parts for easy assembly
  3. With dial on "0" at red marker, separate knob assembly from case assembly. DO NOT interchange knob assembly from one lock with case assembly from another
  4. With one hand, line up two holes in case assembly, with screw holes on inside of locker door
  5. With other hand, insert knob assembly (with red dial marker up and dial at "0") through holes on locker door into case assembly. If units do not fit together flush, turn knob until unit drops into position
  6. Place locking nuts on screw bolts and tighten. Lock is now ready to operate on original combination
Installation Instructions (1700 Series)
  1. Position the lock, inside the locker, so that the two holes on the case align with the holes on the locker door
  2. Insert the bolts onto the holes from the front of the locker through the door and the case.
  3. Place locking nuts on the bolts and tighten
  4. WARNING: If electric socket wrench is used, do not exert excessive pressure
